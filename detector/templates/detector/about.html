About Orchid Mapping

Orchid Mapping with Deep Learning with Semantic Segmentation is an AI-powered project developed to assist in analyzing aerial or drone-captured top-view images of forest regions to identify and count orchid trees. This is done using a semantic segmentation algorithm â€” specifically, a U-Net deep learning model â€” to accurately distinguish trees from the background and estimate their count.

Advanced Technology

Our U-Net model is specifically trained to recognize orchid tree characteristics, achieving over 70% accuracy in identifying orchid trees, even in dense plantations with overlapping canopies.

This tool is designed for ecological researchers, forest department officials, and environmental conservationists who aim to monitor orchid tree growth and distribution more efficiently. With just one image, our model can provide fast and accurate insights.

Our Mission

To provide conservationists and researchers with powerful tools for monitoring orchid populations, helping to preserve these important species through accurate data collection and analysis.

This web application is developed using Django for the backend and modern web technologies for the frontend, ensuring a responsive and user-friendly experience across all devices.
